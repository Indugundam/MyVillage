About Karimaddela

Karimaddula, one of the oldest villages in the Gadivemula mandal of Nandyal district, Andhra Pradesh, is known for its cultural heritage and serene landscapes. With a population of 3,881 (Census 2011), the village thrives on agriculture and community-driven living. It is part of the Panyam Assembly Constituency and Nandyal Parliamentary Constituency.

The village is surrounded by lush green fields and peaceful surroundings, making it a beautiful blend of tradition and natural beauty.


Karimaddela, one of the oldest and most historically rich villages in the Gadivemula mandal of the Nandyal district in Andhra Pradesh, India, is a testament to the enduring spirit of rural India. With its roots tracing back generations, this village carries a legacy of culture and tradition while embracing the nuances of modern governance. As of the 2011 Census, Karimaddula has a population of 3,881, reflecting a close-knit community that thrives on agricultural pursuits and a lifestyle deeply connected to nature. Politically, the village holds significance as it falls under the Panyam Assembly Constituency and the Nandyal Parliamentary Constituency, making it an integral part of the region's democratic fabric. Despite its small size, Karimaddula embodies the essence of a vibrant rural ecosystem, where simplicity and tradition blend seamlessly with aspirations for progress.
